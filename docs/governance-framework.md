# Data Governance Lab — Governance Framework

Version: 0.1  
Status: Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

The Data Governance Framework defines the capabilities used by Data Governance Lab to generate, assess, and evaluate governance scenarios.

The framework represents the knowledge model behind the simulation.

It is designed to reflect how enterprise data governance is practiced in real organizations.

---

# 2. Governance Philosophy

Data governance is the combination of:

- People
- Processes
- Policies
- Technology
- Accountability

The objective of governance is not control for its own sake.

The objective is to enable organizations to create value from trusted, understood, and responsibly managed data.

---

# 3. Governance Capability Model

## 3.1 Strategy & Operating Model

### Purpose

Define how governance operates within the organization.

### Key Questions

- Why does governance exist?
- Who makes governance decisions?
- How are priorities defined?
- How does governance align with business objectives?

### Common Challenges

- No governance strategy
- Governance seen as an IT initiative
- Lack of executive sponsorship
- Unclear decision processes

### Typical Deliverables

- Governance strategy
- Governance charter
- Operating model
- Governance roadmap

---

# 3.2 Organization & Accountability

### Purpose

Establish clear ownership and responsibility for data.

### Key Questions

- Who is accountable for data?
- Who maintains definitions?
- Who approves changes?

### Common Challenges

- IT considered owner of all data
- Business ownership unclear
- Stewardship responsibilities not defined

### Typical Deliverables

- Data ownership model
- RACI matrix
- Governance council structure

---

# 3.3 Metadata Management

### Purpose

Enable understanding of data assets through business and technical metadata.

### Key Questions

- What data exists?
- Where does it come from?
- Who uses it?
- What does it mean?

### Common Challenges

- Unknown datasets
- Poor documentation
- No lineage visibility

### Typical Deliverables

- Data catalog
- Metadata standards
- Lineage processes

---

# 3.4 Business Glossary

### Purpose

Create shared understanding of business terms.

### Key Questions

- What does this term mean?
- Who owns this definition?
- Which systems use it?

### Common Challenges

- Conflicting definitions
- Unmanaged KPIs
- Department-specific terminology

### Typical Deliverables

- Business glossary
- KPI definitions
- Approval workflow

---

# 3.5 Data Quality Management

### Purpose

Ensure data is fit for business use.

### Quality Dimensions

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Consistency
- Timeliness
- Uniqueness
- Validity

### Common Challenges

- Quality issues discovered too late
- No ownership
- Manual reconciliation

### Typical Deliverables

- Quality rules
- Quality monitoring
- Issue management process

---

# 3.6 Data Ownership & Stewardship

### Purpose

Define who manages data throughout its lifecycle.

### Key Concepts

Data Owner:
Accountable business leader.

Data Steward:
Person responsible for day-to-day data management activities.

Data Custodian:
Technical role managing systems and controls.

### Common Challenges

- Stewardship treated as extra work
- Ownership confused with technical responsibility

---

# 3.7 Data Lifecycle Management

### Purpose

Manage data from creation to retirement.

### Key Questions

- How long should data exist?
- When should it be archived?
- When should it be deleted?

### Typical Deliverables

- Retention policies
- Lifecycle processes
- Archiving standards

---

# 3.8 Data Classification & Privacy

### Purpose

Ensure data is handled appropriately according to sensitivity and regulations.

### Common Challenges

- Unknown sensitive data
- Poor classification
- Privacy risks

### Typical Deliverables

- Classification model
- Privacy controls
- Handling standards

---

# 3.9 Data Access Management

### Purpose

Ensure appropriate access to data.

### Governance Questions

- Who should access data?
- Why do they need access?
- Who approves access?

### Common Challenges

- Excessive access
- Slow approvals
- Lack of accountability

---

# 3.10 Master & Reference Data Management

### Purpose

Create consistency for critical business entities.

Examples:

- Customer
- Product
- Supplier
- Location

### Common Challenges

- Duplicate records
- Multiple sources of truth
- Conflicting identifiers

---

# 3.11 Data Architecture Alignment

### Purpose

Ensure governance aligns with enterprise architecture.

### Common Challenges

- Governance disconnected from platforms
- No understanding of data flows
- Poor integration between business and technology

---

# 3.12 Governance Adoption & Change Management

### Purpose

Ensure governance becomes part of normal business behavior.

### Common Challenges

- Resistance
- Lack of awareness
- Governance seen as bureaucracy

### Typical Deliverables

- Training plans
- Communication strategy
- Adoption metrics

# 4. Capability Maturity Levels

## Level 1 — Initial

Characteristics:

- Reactive problem solving
- No formal governance
- Ownership unclear

---

## Level 2 — Developing

Characteristics:

- Some governance activities exist
- Inconsistent practices
- Limited accountability

---

## Level 3 — Defined

Characteristics:

- Formal governance processes
- Clear roles
- Standardized practices

---

## Level 4 — Managed

Characteristics:

- Metrics-driven governance
- Continuous improvement
- Enterprise adoption

---

## Level 5 — Optimized

Characteristics:

- Governance embedded into business operations
- Data enables innovation
- Proactive management

---

# 5. Framework Usage in Simulation

The framework is used to:

- Generate realistic challenges
- Assess governance maturity
- Evaluate user recommendations
- Provide expert feedback

A scenario may involve multiple capabilities, but problems should remain connected to a realistic business objective.

Example:

Business Problem:

Executive reports show conflicting revenue numbers.

Governance capabilities involved:

- Business glossary
- Data ownership
- Data quality
- Metadata management

---

# Conclusion

The Data Governance Lab framework provides the foundation for realistic governance simulations.

The objective is not to test whether users know terminology.

The objective is to test whether users can apply governance principles to solve real business problems.
